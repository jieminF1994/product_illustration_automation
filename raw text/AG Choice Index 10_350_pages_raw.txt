0003: Basic facts about index annuities
0004: • Index annuities are long-term contracts.
0005: • You do not invest directly in the securities and other investments comprising the index.
0006: • Index interest is not guaranteed.
0007: • Factors determining degree of participation in index performance (e.g., caps) may limit interest earned, can be reset each index term and can be lower in future periods.
0008: • Contracts with living benefits have rider fees that reduce contract value as long as the rider is in effect; deduction of these fees reduces amount of interest you derive
0009: from the index option.
0010: • Constituent investments in a "dynamic" index can change over time based on the guiding index strategy, and there is no guarantee that such changes will produce
0011: positive performance or will mirror changes in essentially static indices like the S&P 500.
0012: An important word about the assumptions underlying the illustrated values and how illustrations are merely hypothetical
0013: First, it is important to note that hypothetical illustrations are intended to help you understand how this annuity product works in broad terms. In general, actual performance of
0014: the index crediting strategies is utilized, and these figures are combined with current crediting rates in order to demonstrate a potential performance path. 1 Many different
0015: factors will cause your actual performance to differ from the illustrated outcomes, such as index performance, initial participation and crediting rates, renewal rates and any
0016: withdrawals taken. It is important to note that this illustration is not a promise of future returns or results.
0017: Further, we want to highlight some important factors that impact this illustration and should be understood:
0018: • Historical performance: Generally, market performance during the 2010’s was particularly strong, and this illustration utilizes much of the performance from that time period.
0019: While that type of performance could continue, past performance is not indicative of future results, and different volatility and/or interest rate environments can result in
0020: different performance results of any index in the future.
0021: • Impact of Market Interest Rates And Other Factors. A number of variables are taken into consideration when we set crediting rates like participation rates and trigger rates.
0022: Those factors include prevailing interest rates in relevant markets, changes in competitor rates, and the withdrawal charge status of the annuity contract. Crediting rates that
0023: have increased provide you with a greater opportunity for higher interest credits, but still could result in zero interest credits if the underlying index performance is not
0024: positive. Similarly, lower crediting rates provide you with less opportunity than during higher interest rate periods, but still could result in zero interest credits if the underlying
0025: index performance is not positive.
0026: Also be aware that current crediting rates are held constant for the entire period reflected in the illustration. Please keep in mind that your actual crediting rates can change (lower
0027: or higher) as frequently as annually and will impact the actual outcome.
0028: Therefore, as you work with your financial professional to determine what is best for you, please be mindful of this information and do not overly rely upon the illustrated outcomes
0029: when setting your expectations for future results. Your actual results will be determined based on the future performance of the index you choose and the initial and renewal
0030: crediting rates set on the annuity, among other factors.

0006: charges and market value adjustment (MVA). If your premium is tax-qualified (such as an IRA), an annuity provides no additional tax-deferred benefit beyond that provided by the plan. You should
0007: only use a fixed-index annuity in a tax-qualified plan if you want to benefit from features other than tax deferral. Please consult with your financial and tax advisor regarding your individual situation.
0008: Guarantees are backed by the claims paying ability of the issuing insurance company.
0009: This hypothetical illustration is provided to give you a feel for the interest crediting that would result from each of the interest crediting strategies based on the history or back-tested history of the
0010: related indices. Allocating money to these options does not represent an investment directly in the index. AG Choice Index 10 offers interest accounts, each with a different interest crediting
0011: strategy; see Understanding Your Interest Crediting Strategies on page 14 for details. These accounts are subject to change until the contract is issued and once issued, they may be discontinued or
0012: changed with prior notice.

0014: withdrawals. The annual percentage of the Income Base that you receive for life depends on the number of people covered and the covered person(s) age(s) at the time of activation of lifetime
0015: withdrawals. The rider offers you the opportunity to earn annual income credits equal to 200% of the rate of interest earned prior to activating lifetime income and 100% of the rate of interest earned
0016: after lifetime income begins. You even have the opportunity to change the number of individuals covered under the rider (Single or Joint Life) to help meet varying income needs, or to address life

0028: Back-tested performance of certain indices
0029: Important Information Regarding Back-Tested Performance of the ML Strategic Balanced Index, PIMCO Global Optima Index, Dimensional US Foundations Index, and Invesco New Economy Index:
0030: Performance of these indices, some or all of which may be available under this annuity, including but not limited to hypothetical, back-tested performance, is not necessarily an indication of future
0031: results. Back-tested performance is not actual historical performance, but as a general rule is derived by applying actual past performance of the index to conditions that were present in the relevant
0032: markets during a time period preceding the inception of the index. These back-tested calculations generally are done based on the same methodology used today to derive the actual performance
0033: of the index. Thus, back-tested performance is an estimate of how the index would have performed prior to its inception. The back-test period may not correspond to the entire available history of
0034: the index.
0035: Another limitation of back-tested performance is that, generally, the performance reflects application of the index methodology and selection of index constituents with the benefit of hindsight. No
0036: hypothetical record can completely account for the impact of financial risk associated with real-world events. For example, there are numerous factors related to equities and fixed income securities
0037: which cannot be, and have not been, accounted for in the preparation of the back-tested information.

0003: What you should know about the indices underlying our index crediting strategies
0004: The S&P 500® Index* includes 500 of the leading companies in the U.S. stock market. It is widely regarded as the standard for measuring U.S. stock market performance. The
0005: returns for this index does not include dividends. This means that dividends paid on the securities underlying this index is not included in the index return, and index interest
0006: therefore will not reflect the higher returns that would apply if dividends were included. When allocating money to an interest crediting option that uses this index in the
0007: calculation of interest, a key consideration for the performance of the S&P 500 ® index (excluding dividends) is the change in the value of the Index from the beginning of the
0008: index term to its end.
0009: The ML Strategic Balanced Index ® (MLSB) blends equity and fixed income indices using a non-discretionary, rules-based process to adjust exposures to the S&P 500® Index
0010: and the Merrill Lynch 10-Year U.S. Treasury Futures Total Return Index. This process may help the Index generate positive returns while meeting a target volatility level. Pursuant
0011: to the rules of the index, the index weightings are rebalanced every six months based on the historical volatility of the Index. As an additional measure of risk control, the index
0012: methodology may cause increases or decreases in cash positions daily to help maintain the overall volatility of the Index at or near its targeted 6%. Volatility control measures seek
0013: to provide smoother results and mitigate sharp market fluctuations. While this type of strategy can lessen the impact of market downturns, it is important to note that it may also
0014: lessen the impact of market upturns, therefore limiting upside potential. When allocating money to an interest crediting option that uses this index in the calculation of interest,
0015: some key considerations for the performance of the underlying index include the change in the S&P 500 Index (excluding dividends) over the index term, the change in the
0016: Merrill Lynch 10-Year U.S. Treasury Futures Total Return Index over the index term, the weightings assigned to the indices (e.g. target volatility mechanism), the absolute level of
0017: interest rates, any directional move in interest rate levels, interest rate volatility, and stock market volatility
0018: The PIMCO Global Optima Index ® (PIMCO)* is designed to combine diversified market exposure with enhanced return potential. It allocates to a broad range of global equity
0019: and U.S. fixed income markets while targeting a moderate, consistent risk profile. The equity portion optimizes across U.S. large cap, U.S. mid cap, U.S. small cap, international and
0020: emerging markets. Specifically, it seeks to enhance returns by dynamically adjusting its allocation across these markets, favoring those markets that exhibit positive momentum
0021: and that are likely to offer value. The fixed income portion invests in high-grade U.S. bonds, including Treasuries, corporate bonds, and mortgage-backed securities. In order to
0022: manage risk, the index adjusts the mix of equity and fixed income on a daily basis in order to meet a target volatility level of 7.5% annualized
0023: The Russell 2000® Index* is an equity index that measures the performance of the 2,000 smallest companies in the Russell 3000® Index, which is made up of 3,000 of the
0024: biggest U.S. stocks. The Russell 2000® Index is constructed to provide a comprehensive and unbiased small-cap barometer and is completely reconstituted annually to ensure
0025: larger stocks do not affect the performance and characteristics of the true small-cap index.
0026: The MSCI EAFE Index* is designed to represent the performance of large-and mid-cap securities across 21 developed markets in Europe, Australasia, Far East, excluding the US
0027: & Canada. As of September 2018, it had more than 900 constituents and covered approximately 85% of the free float-adjusted market capitalization in each country.
0028: The Franklin Quality Dividend Index provides exposure to a combination of ETFs and cash or cash-equivalents. The ETFs utilized invest in U.S. large-cap stocks that tend to pay
0029: relatively high dividends or have low price volatility.
0030: *The returns for these indices do not include dividends. This means that dividends paid on the securities underlying these indices are not included in the index return for each
0031: index, and index interest therefore will not reflect the higher returns that would apply if dividends were included.
