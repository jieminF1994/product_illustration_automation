0003: Basic facts about index annuities
0004: • Index annuities are long-term contracts.
0005: • You do not invest directly in the securities and other investments comprising the index.
0006: • Index interest is not guaranteed.
0007: • Factors determining degree of participation in index performance (e.g., caps) may limit interest earned, can be reset each index term and can be lower in future periods.
0008: • Constituent investments in a "dynamic" index can change over time based on the guiding index strategy, and there is no guarantee that such changes will produce
0009: positive performance or will mirror changes in essentially static indices like the S&P 500.
0010: An important word about the assumptions underlying the illustrated values and how illustrations are merely hypothetical
0011: First, it is important to note that hypothetical illustrations are intended to help you understand how this annuity product works in broad terms. In general, actual and/or back-
0012: tested performance of the index crediting strategies is utilized, and these figures are combined with current crediting rates in order to demonstrate a potential performance path.
0013: 1 Many different factors will cause your actual performance to differ from the illustrated outcomes, such as index performance, initial participation and crediting rates, renewal
0014: rates and any withdrawals taken. It is important to note that this illustration is not a promise of future returns or results.
0015: Further, we want to highlight some important factors that impact this illustration and should be understood:
0016: • Historical performance: Generally, market performance during the 2010’s was particularly strong, and this illustration utilizes much of the performance from that time period.
0017: While that type of performance could continue, past performance is not indicative of future results, and different volatility and/or interest rate environments can result in
0018: different performance results of any index in the future.
0019: • Impact of Market Interest Rates And Other Factors. A number of variables are taken into consideration when we set crediting rates like participation rates and trigger rates.
0020: Those factors include prevailing interest rates in relevant markets, changes in competitor rates, and the withdrawal charge status of the annuity contract. Crediting rates that
0021: have increased provide you with a greater opportunity for higher interest credits, but still could result in zero interest credits if the underlying index performance is not
0022: positive. Similarly, lower crediting rates provide you with less opportunity than during higher interest rate periods, but still could result in zero interest credits if the underlying
0023: index performance is not positive.
0024: Also be aware that current crediting rates are held constant for the entire period reflected in the illustration. Please keep in mind that your actual crediting rates can change (lower
0025: or higher) as frequently as annually and will impact the actual outcome.
0026: Therefore, as you work with your financial professional to determine what is best for you, please be mindful of this information and do not overly rely upon the illustrated outcomes
0027: when setting your expectations for future results. Your actual results will be determined based on the future performance of the index you choose and the initial and renewal
0028: crediting rates set on the annuity, among other factors.
0029: Back Tested Performance: In general, the values in these illustrations take the past returns of your chosen index and then assume hypothetically that those returns are attained in the future time periods depicted. If an index
0030: has not existed for the entire period, we may use “back-tested” performance for years where actual historical performance is not available. Thus, back-tested performance is an estimate of how the index would have
0031: performed prior to its inception. Back-tested performance may not be indicative of how the investment will perform long-term, and can result in illustrated values that are extraordinarily high. To set reasonable expectations
0032: for performance of indices that rely on back-tested results, in certain illustrations we limit the credited interest rate in any given year to the average annual return for the S&P 500 over the last 30 years, which currently is
0033: 10.50%.

0029: results. Back-tested performance is not actual historical performance, but as a general rule is derived by applying actual past performance of the index to conditions that were present in the relevant
0030: markets during a time period preceding the inception of the index. These back-tested calculations generally are done based on the same methodology used today to derive the actual performance

0003: What you should know about the indices underlying our index crediting strategies
0004: The S&P 500® Index* includes 500 of the leading companies in the U.S. stock market. It is widely regarded as the standard for measuring U.S. stock market performance. The
0005: returns for this index does not include dividends. This means that dividends paid on the securities underlying this index is not included in the index return, and index interest
0006: therefore will not reflect the higher returns that would apply if dividends were included. When allocating money to an interest crediting option that uses this index in the
0007: calculation of interest, a key consideration for the performance of the S&P 500 ® index (excluding dividends) is the change in the value of the Index from the beginning of the
0008: index term to its end.
0009: The ML Strategic Balanced Index ® (MLSB) blends equity and fixed income indices using a non-discretionary, rules-based process to adjust exposures to the S&P 500® Index
0010: and the Merrill Lynch 10-Year U.S. Treasury Futures Total Return Index. This process may help the Index generate positive returns while meeting a target volatility level. Pursuant
0011: to the rules of the index, the index weightings are rebalanced every six months based on the historical volatility of the Index. As an additional measure of risk control, the index
0012: methodology may cause increases or decreases in cash positions daily to help maintain the overall volatility of the Index at or near its targeted 6%. Volatility control measures seek
0013: to provide smoother results and mitigate sharp market fluctuations. While this type of strategy can lessen the impact of market downturns, it is important to note that it may also
0014: lessen the impact of market upturns, therefore limiting upside potential. When allocating money to an interest crediting option that uses this index in the calculation of interest,
0015: some key considerations for the performance of the underlying index include the change in the S&P 500 Index (excluding dividends) over the index term, the change in the
0016: Merrill Lynch 10-Year U.S. Treasury Futures Total Return Index over the index term, the weightings assigned to the indices (e.g. target volatility mechanism), the absolute level of
0017: interest rates, any directional move in interest rate levels, interest rate volatility, and stock market volatility
0018: The PIMCO Global Optima Index ® (PIMCO)* is designed to combine diversified market exposure with enhanced return potential. It allocates to a broad range of global equity
0019: and U.S. fixed income markets while targeting a moderate, consistent risk profile. The equity portion optimizes across U.S. large cap, U.S. mid cap, U.S. small cap, international and
0020: emerging markets. Specifically, it seeks to enhance returns by dynamically adjusting its allocation across these markets, favoring those markets that exhibit positive momentum
0021: and that are likely to offer value. The fixed income portion invests in high-grade U.S. bonds, including Treasuries, corporate bonds, and mortgage-backed securities. In order to
0022: manage risk, the index adjusts the mix of equity and fixed income on a daily basis in order to meet a target volatility level of 7.5% annualized
0023: Nasdaq-100® Index* includes 100 of the largest domestic and international non-financial companies listed on The Nasdaq Stock Market based on market capitalization. The
0024: Index reflects companies across major industry groups including computer hardware and software, telecommunications, retail/wholesale trade and biotechnology. It does not
0025: contain securities of financial companies including investment companies.
0026: The Dimensional US Foundations Index is a rules-based multi-asset index that aims to provide higher expected returns in each asset class by leveraging Dimensional’ s 40+ years
0027: of experience in financial science research and application. The Index allocates exposure on a daily basis among Equities, Fixed Income, and Commodities with a 5% volatility
0028: target using a combination of forward looking implied volatility measures informed by joint research of Dimensional with Nobel Prize Laureate Robert C. Merton and truVol® from
0029: Salt Financial, which uses historical intraday data.
0030: The Invesco New Economy Index is a target volatility index, which seeks to provide strong risk adjusted returns while maintaining a 12.5% volatility target. The starting point of
0031: the index allocates to a combination of exchange traded products (ETPs) targeting a ratio of 80% innovation-focused equity exposure and 20% Bitcoin exposure, when index
0032: volatility is 12.5%, while using non-remunerated cash2 positions to adjust index volatility down when the target is exceeded. The equity and Bitcoin effective weights will vary
0033: depending on volatility but will seek to maintain a targeted ratio of 80%/20%. Changes in volatility in each component will dictate the final weighting of the index daily, which may
0034: vary significantly from the starting point of 80% innovation-focused equity exposure and 20% Bitcoin exposure, particularly in periods of higher volatility when cash2 is being
0035: utilized to dampen index volatility.
0036: The first pillar of the Invesco New Economy Index is the Invesco QQQ exchange traded fund (ETF). The Invesco QQQ ETF is designed to track the Nasdaq-100 Index® providing
0037: investors exposure to the 100 largest non-financial companies listed on the Nasdaq. The second pillar of the index is the Invesco Galaxy Bitcoin ETF (BTCO). BTCO aims to track
0038: the spot price of Bitcoin and provides investors with a secure and simpler access point to cryptocurrency. Bitcoin is an alternative to the traditional banking system. Bitcoin is
0039: maintained using a decentralized ledger system known as a blockchain. The value of bitcoin, like the value of other digital assets, is not backed by any government, corporation, or
0040: other identified body, unlike traditional currencies like the US dollar.
0041: With the goal of delivering a more stable performance profile over time, the Index’s exposure to equities, Bitcoin and cash2 is adjusted daily with the aim to achieve a target
0042: volatility of 12.5% annually1. When the riskiness of stock and/or Bitcoin holdings rises, the index will shift away from stocks and Bitcoin and into cash2. Or as the riskiness of stock
0043: and Bitcoin holdings decreases, the index will shift away from cash and into stocks and Bitcoin.
0044: 1. There is no guarantee the stated volatility target will be achieved.
0045: 2. The cash position is nonremunerated, which means that the amount of readily available cash does not directly generate income or provide any financial return.
